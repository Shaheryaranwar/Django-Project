Spaghetti Carbonara

Prep: 15 mins Cook: 15 mins Total: 30 mins

Description

A classic Italian pasta dish made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and black pepper. This creamy, rich spaghetti carbonara recipe is surprisingly easy to make at home with just a few simple ingredients and our step-by-step instructions.

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 200g pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 4 large eggs
  • 50g pecorino cheese, grated
  • 50g parmesan, grated
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 garlic clove (optional)
  • Salt for pasta water

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to boil and cook spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining.
  2. While pasta cooks, heat a large skillet over medium heat. Add pancetta and cook until crispy, about 7-8 minutes. Remove from heat and set aside.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, grated cheeses, and plenty of black pepper.
  4. Working quickly, add drained hot pasta to the pancetta in the skillet. Remove from heat.
  5. Pour the egg mixture over the pasta, tossing continuously with tongs. Add reserved pasta water a tablespoon at a time until you get a creamy sauce that coats the pasta.
  6. Serve immediately with extra grated cheese and black pepper on top.

Notes

  • For authentic carbonara, use guanciale if available
  • The heat from the pasta cooks the eggs - be careful not to scramble them
  • Freshly grated cheese is essential for the best texture
  • Serve immediately as the sauce thickens as it cools
